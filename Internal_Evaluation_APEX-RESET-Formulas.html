Introduction

This report provides a scientific analysis of the APEX and RESET supplement formulas – a complementary pair designed for daytime cognitive enhancement (APEX) and nighttime recovery (RESET). APEX is recommended on a 5-days-on, 2-days-off weekly cycle as a nootropic stack for sustained focus and "clean" energy, while RESET is intended for daily use in the evening to promote deep sleep and holistic recovery.

The goal is to evaluate each ingredient's evidence base (human and animal studies), mechanisms of action (neurotransmitter, neurotrophic, metabolic, vascular pathways), relevant experiments, anecdotal reports, and dosage considerations. We also assess potential synergistic effects when using APEX and RESET together, considering neuroscience, metabolic, and chronobiological perspectives (e.g. whether alternating stimulation and recovery enhances neuroplasticity, hormonal resilience, and performance consistency).

Formula Overview

It provides stimulants (150 mg caffeine from coffee & anhydrous, plus 100 mg theobromine) balanced with calming nootropic amino acids (300 mg L-theanine) to yield calm focus rather than jittery energy. It includes cholinergic support (Alpha-GPC, Citicoline, Uridine) for membrane and neurotransmitter synthesis, neurotrophic factors and antioxidants (Lion’s Mane, Magnesium L-threonate, PQQ, Lutein) for brain plasticity and protection, dopamine precursors & adaptogens (Tyrosine, Rhodiola) for stress resilience, and metabolic/vascular enhancers (MCT oil, Inulin, high-flavanol Cocoa, Cinnamon, Pine Bark extract, BioPerine) for sustained energy metabolism and cerebral blood flow[3][4].

The recommended regimen is 5 days on, 2 days off to maintain sensitivity and avoid tolerance[5] – a cycle often advised for stimulant-heavy nootropics[6][7].

Calm Focus & Stimulation: Caffeine (150 mg), L-Theanine (300 mg), Theobromine (100 mg)

This trio provides the cornerstone of APEX’s energizing yet focused cognitive boost by combining a primary stimulant (caffeine) with a calming amino acid (L-theanine) and a milder, longer-lasting stimulant (theobromine) to achieve a state of "calm energy" conducive to productivity.

This trio provides the cornerstone of APEX’s energizing yet focused cognitive boost. Caffeine is a well-established stimulant that antagonizes adenosine receptors, thereby reducing drowsiness and enhancing neuronal firing[51]. At 150 mg total (roughly equivalent to a strong cup of coffee), caffeine reliably improves alertness, reaction time, and vigilance[52][53], especially in fatigued states. It can also enhance certain memory and processing speed measures[9]. However, chronic daily caffeine use can lead to tolerance as the body down-regulates adenosine receptors[54][7]. The 5-days-on/2-days-off cycle is intended to mitigate tolerance build-up, preserving caffeine’s efficacy over time[6][55]. This cycling strategy aligns with nootropic best practices: stimulant benefits are maintained without escalating doses, since periodic off-days allow receptor sensitivity to reset[7].

Importantly, APEX pairs caffeine with L-Theanine, an amino acid from tea that promotes calm attentiveness. L-theanine readily crosses the BBB and modulates brain chemistry by increasing GABA and alpha-wave activity, which smooths out caffeine’s stimulatory effects (reducing jitters and anxiety)[56][12]. Human studies show that 200–400 mg L-theanine alone can improve attention and reaction time in high-anxiety individuals[57], and when combined with caffeine it enhances task accuracy and subjective focus more than caffeine alone[58]. In APEX, 300 mg is a substantial dose in line with effective amounts. L-theanine’s neuroprotective angle is notable as well – animal models indicate it can reduce stress-induced cognitive impairment and even promote hippocampal neurogenesis[59][60]. Anecdotally, many users report the caffeine+theanine combo yields a state of “calm energy”, supporting prolonged concentration without the crash or anxiety often associated with caffeine alone.

Theobromine, at 100 mg, is the third component of this stimulant cluster. Theobromine is the primary methylxanthine in cocoa (structurally similar to caffeine but less potent on CNS). It acts as a mild, long-lasting stimulant and vasodilator. While theobromine’s cognitive effects are not as pronounced as caffeine’s, it can contribute to mental alertness and improved mood in a gentle manner[14]. Notably, theobromine has a longer half-life and fewer peripheral side effects (e.g. less impact on blood pressure) than caffeine. Research indicates it may have neuroprotective and cognitive benefits at moderate intake, possibly mitigating age-related decline[61]. However, very high doses can cause headache or nausea (as seen in some chocolate overdose case studies), so keeping it at 100 mg (roughly what’s found in ~5–6 g of cocoa) provides a margin of safety. In sum, theobromine likely complements caffeine by prolonging the stimulatory effect (as caffeine tapers, theobromine’s milder stimulation and vasodilation sustain cerebral blood flow).

The synergy of these three is evident: Caffeine provides the primary drive (rapid increase in alertness and energy), theanine provides anxiolytic and alpha-wave tuning to maintain clarity and calm[56], and theobromine extends stimulation and supports blood flow. This results in a focused yet relaxed cognitive state conducive to productivity. From a mechanistic view, caffeine and theobromine inhibit adenosine (promoting wakefulness) and raise catecholamines, while theanine increases GABA and modulates glutamate receptors – a balancing act. Human EEG studies confirm that theanine (100–200 mg) increases alpha-band brain waves associated with relaxed focus[62], and when co-administered with caffeine, subjects show improved attention switching and reduced mental fatigue[58]. Thus, APEX’s stimulant module is strongly evidence-backed, combining ingredients that have individually and jointly demonstrated cognitive benefits in controlled trials.

Dosage Relevance: 150 mg caffeine is within typical nootropic use (moderate – roughly equivalent to 1.5 cups of coffee). 300 mg L-theanine is at the high end of common supplemental doses (most studies use 100–250 mg). This high dose is justified by literature showing clear benefits for stress reduction and sleep quality in that range[13]; in daytime use it may blunt excess caffeine stimulation effectively. 100 mg theobromine is a relatively low dose (consider that 70% dark chocolate bar provides ~300 mg) to add subtle effect without side effects. These amounts are likely safe for continuous use, and the 2-day breaks each week further ensure the HPA axis isn’t chronically overstimulated. Overall, the stimulant portion of APEX aligns with known efficacious doses and ratios (the caffeine:theanine ~1:2 ratio is a popular evidence-based approach).

Cholinergic Support: Alpha-GPC (300 mg), Citicoline (250 mg), Uridine Monophosphate (200 mg)

APEX includes a robust cholinergic stack to support acetylcholine (ACh) synthesis and cell membrane health, which are critical for memory, learning, and executive function. The combination aims to enhance not just acute focus but also long-term brain structure.

APEX includes a robust cholinergic stack to support acetylcholine (ACh) synthesis and cell membrane health, which are critical for memory, learning, and executive function. Alpha-GPC (α-glycerylphosphorylcholine) at 300 mg is a highly bioavailable choline source that readily crosses into the brain and directly donates choline for ACh production[63]. In Europe, Alpha-GPC is actually used as a prescription cognitive enhancer for stroke recovery and dementia at higher doses (1,200 mg/day); those trials have shown improved cognitive scores in Alzheimer’s and vascular dementia patients[16]. At 300 mg, we’re in the range commonly used in nootropic supplements for healthy individuals. One acute study in young males found that 250–500 mg Alpha-GPC significantly improved immediate memory and attention on a computerized test battery[17]. Mechanistically, beyond supplying ACh building blocks, alpha-GPC also triggers the release of calcium and dopamine in the brain, and some research suggests it can stimulate growth hormone release in exercise contexts. Moreover, rodent studies indicate anti-amnesic effects – e.g. alpha-GPC reverses scopolamine-induced memory deficits[64]. The presence of alpha-GPC in APEX should help optimize short-term memory (working memory) and mental clarity, especially under high cognitive load when acetylcholine can be depleted.

Citicoline (CDP-Choline) at 250 mg complements alpha-GPC by providing both choline and cytidine (which converts to uridine in vivo). Citicoline supports the phosphatidylcholine synthesis in neuronal membranes and increases levels of neurotransmitters (ACh, dopamine). Notably, citicoline has human data demonstrating cognitive benefits: a 12-week RCT in middle-aged adults with memory complaints found 500 mg/day citicoline improved episodic memory and overall memory performance compared to placebo[19]. Another trial in adolescent males showed improved attention and fewer impulsivity errors on citicoline vs placebo (though sample sizes were small). Citicoline is also neuroprotective in ischemic stroke and TBI, likely by stabilizing membranes and reducing inflammation[65]. At 250 mg, APEX’s dose is in line with cognitive enhancement studies (250–500 mg/day). The combination of alpha-GPC and citicoline is somewhat redundant in terms of providing choline, but each has unique metabolites (alpha-GPC yields glycerophosphate for cell structures; citicoline yields uridine). Together they ensure robust cholinergic tone for sustained focus and memory encoding. Anecdotally, users often report that citicoline gives a clean “mental energy” and motivation lift (possibly via mild dopamine upregulation), which aligns with findings that CDP-choline raises dopamine in the frontal cortex[66].

Additionally, Uridine Monophosphate (200 mg) is included to synergize with citicoline. Uridine is a nucleotide that, once converted to UTP and CTP, is crucial for synthesizing neuronal membranes and synaptic proteins. It also indirectly supports dopamine receptor sensitivity. There is an interesting body of work (mostly preclinical and anecdotal) on the “uridine + DHA + choline” combination enhancing synaptogenesis. For example, animal studies by Wurtman’s group showed this combo increased dendritic spine density and cognitive function in rodents. Human evidence is sparser; however, uridine has been studied in depression (1 g/day uridine had antidepressant effects in bipolar patients in a small trial). At 200 mg, the dose is modest but likely sufficient to elevate plasma uridine. This could help with phospholipid synthesis in brain cell membranes, complementing citicoline’s pathway[67]. Uridine also may amplify the cholinergic support: when neurons have ample uridine, choline, and DHA, they can form CDP-choline endogenously and incorporate it into new phosphatidylcholine for synaptic membranes[67]. In a nutshell, APEX’s cholinergic/ phospholipid support should enhance memory, focus, and even motivation by ensuring the brain has the raw materials for neurotransmitter release and synaptic formation.

Mechanistically, these ingredients activate multiple pathways: Alpha-GPC and Citicoline both boost acetylcholine (improving signaling in memory-related circuits), while Uridine (with cytidine from citicoline) boosts synaptic membrane synthesis and possibly neurite outgrowth. The inclusion of all three is aggressive but scientifically grounded for long-term cognitive health. Notably, this could also promote long-term potentiation (LTP) capacity – the cellular basis of learning – by supplying ample membrane constituents for forming new synapses (which is congruent with the product’s neuroplasticity claims[68]).

Dosage and Evidence: Combining cholinergics is generally safe; these substances are naturally present in diets (e.g. citicoline in organ meats, alpha-GPC as a byproduct of phosphatidylcholine metabolism). The doses here are backed by research: 250 mg citicoline showed benefits in healthy older adults[19], 300–600 mg alpha-GPC improves cognitive processing speed in trials[17], and although uridine 200 mg doesn’t have standalone human trials, it is in the range used in metabolic studies as a supplement. We should note one caveat: excessive cholinergic activity can cause headaches or low mood in some individuals (the so-called “choline blues”), but the amounts here are moderate and the 2-days-off cycle would help avoid any buildup. Overall, the cholinergic stack in APEX is a forward-thinking inclusion aimed at supporting not just acute focus but also brain structure and function over time.

Neuroplasticity & Neuroprotection: Magnesium L-Threonate, Lion's Mane, Lutein, PQQ

These ingredients target the enhancement of brain plasticity, neurogenesis, and long-term brain health. They work through different mechanisms to support cognitive longevity and the brain’s adaptive capacity.

These ingredients in APEX target the enhancement of brain plasticity, neurogenesis, and long-term brain health. They each work through different mechanisms (ion regulation, neurotrophic factors, antioxidant protection, etc.) to support cognitive longevity and the brain’s adaptive capacity.

Magnesium L-Threonate (Magtein®) stands out as a form of magnesium designed to cross the blood-brain barrier effectively, raising magnesium levels in the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex[69]. Magnesium is a cofactor for many neuronal enzymes and modulates NMDA receptors; sufficient brain Mg²⁺ is known to stabilize synapses and facilitate learning-induced synaptic plasticity. A landmark animal study in Neuron (2010) showed that elevating brain magnesium via Mg L-threonate enhanced learning, working memory, and synaptic density in aged rats[70][22]. Building on that, a recent double-blind trial in healthy adults (18–65) using a magnesium L-threonate-based supplement (combined with vitamin D and phosphatidylserine) found significant improvements in multiple memory measures after 1 month, particularly in older participants[23][71]. Participants taking Mg L-threonate improved in subtests of the Chinese Clinical Memory Test compared to placebo, suggesting real cognitive benefits. Notably, older subjects saw greater improvement, aligning with the idea that restoring brain Mg²⁺ helps reverse age-related cognitive decline[72]. The likely mechanism is that magnesium enhances synaptic plasticity (e.g. promoting Long-Term Potentiation in hippocampal circuits). Additionally, magnesium has a calming effect on the nervous system (as it antagonizes NMDA excitotoxicity and supports GABA function), which might contribute to stress resilience during intense cognitive work. At 1,650 mg Magtein (providing ~108 mg elemental Mg), APEX provides about 1/3 of the RDA for Mg but more importantly, it’s targeting the brain. This dose is in line with the early human study (they used 1,000–2,000 mg Magtein daily). We consider this a strong inclusion: higher brain magnesium correlates with better cognitive function in older adults[73], and supplementation can potentially “rejuvenate” aspects of brain function (one small study in people with cognitive impairment reported an effective reversal of cognitive age by about 9 years with Mg L-threonate, though more research is needed).

Lion’s Mane Mushroom (Hericium erinaceus) at 1 gram (8:1 extract) is APEX’s natural neurotrophic component. Lion’s Mane contains hericenones and erinacines which can stimulate the production of Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) in the brain[74]. This is unique among medicinal plants. A well-known double-blind trial in Japan (Mori et al., 2009) in 30 older adults with mild cognitive impairment showed that 4 capsules of Lion’s Mane (total 3 g extract/day) over 16 weeks significantly improved cognitive scores on the MMSE compared to placebo[25]. The effect waned after stopping intake (scores declined at the 4-week follow-up), implying Lion’s Mane was actively supporting cognitive function. Another placebo-controlled trial in 50- to 80-year-olds (Saitsu et al., 2019) saw some improvement in cognitive function with 12 weeks of Lion’s Mane, though effects were limited to one out of three cognitive tests[75]. In younger adults, results have been mixed or negligible on acute dosing[76] – because the benefits of Lion’s Mane accrue with continuous use as it induces neurotrophic factors rather than an immediate nootropic effect. Preclinical studies are quite promising: Lion’s Mane extracts reduce β-amyloid plaque burden and inflammation in mouse models of Alzheimer’s, and promote neurite outgrowth in neuronal cell cultures[27]. These suggest a potential disease-modifying effect if used long-term. The 1 g 8:1 extract in APEX is roughly equivalent to 8 g of raw mushroom, which is a substantial daily amount (close to traditional doses). At this dose, we expect enhanced neuroplasticity: possibly improved myelination and nerve growth (there are animal studies showing Lion’s Mane helped regenerate myelin on damaged neurons). Subjectively, users often report Lion’s Mane gives them a subtle increase in mental clarity and creativity after weeks of use – likely due to ongoing support of neuronal health. From an evidence standpoint, Lion’s Mane is one of the few nootropic ingredients that genuinely stimulate nerve growth (at least in vitro and in animals)[77], aligning perfectly with APEX’s goal of long-term brain performance.

Lutein (10 mg) in APEX might seem unusual for a nootropic, but emerging research dubs lutein a “brain carotenoid.” Lutein preferentially accumulates in neural tissue (it makes up a significant portion of the macular pigment in the eye and is found in the brain, especially in regions like the temporal cortex). Its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties can protect neurons from oxidative stress. In older adults, higher lutein levels have been correlated with better cognitive scores and memory[78]. There have been intervention trials: e.g., a study in women aged 60–80 showed that 12 months of lutein (12 mg) plus other nutrients improved cognitive function (verbal fluency and memory) compared to placebo. A 2020 systematic review and an RCT using lutein with DHA also reported improved cognition in older individuals[29]. The mechanistic insight comes from brain imaging studies: 10 mg/day of lutein over a year increased cerebral blood flow in certain regions and improved the brain’s efficiency during learning tasks[28]. Lutein may also preserve the integrity of neural membranes (being fat-soluble and embedding in cell membranes to reduce oxidative damage). In APEX, 10 mg (FloraGLO® is a patented highly bioavailable form) is clinically relevant – it’s the dose often used for eye health trials and cognitive studies. For a knowledge worker or biohacker, lutein could support visual processing (less eye strain, better visual memory) and cognitive function, particularly processing speed, as some studies in young adults suggest improved visual reaction time with lutein+zeaxanthin. It also complements the formula’s antioxidant network (with PQQ, cocoa, pine bark, etc.) by specifically localizing in neural tissue. Considering that diet-derived lutein (from leafy greens, etc.) is often below recommended levels in modern diets[79], supplementation here addresses a potential gap. In summary, lutein in APEX serves as a neuroprotective agent – protecting retina and brain from oxidative stress and potentially aiding memory and processing, especially in synergy with other nutrients.

Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (PQQ, 10 mg) is a potent redox cofactor found in certain foods (like fermented soy) and known for its role in mitochondrial biogenesis. PQQ can activate cell signaling pathways (such as CREB and PGC-1α) that lead to the creation of new mitochondria and enhancement of cellular energy production. In the context of the brain, PQQ is neuroprotective: it scavenges free radicals efficiently (a catalytic antioxidant) and has been shown to protect neurons from glutamate-induced toxicity. Animal research has demonstrated that PQQ supplementation improves memory and cognition in rodent models of aging and oxidative stress, likely by preserving mitochondrial function and stimulating NGF synthesis. For example, one study found PQQ protected against cognitive impairment in an Alzheimer’s mouse model by reducing inflammation and oxidative damage in the brain. Human data on PQQ is still limited but promising: a clinical trial in middle-aged adults found that 20 mg/day PQQ for 12 weeks reduced feelings of fatigue and improved attention compared to placebo, likely via improving mitochondrial metrics. PQQ also improves sleep quality in one small human study (possibly by lowering stress). The dose of 10 mg in APEX is a typical supplemental dose (the range is 10–20 mg in most products). Given its high cost and novelty, including PQQ at all signals an advanced formulation. We expect PQQ in APEX to contribute to mitochondrial health of neurons, thereby supporting sustained cognitive energy and protecting against long-term neurodegeneration. It works well alongside CoQ10 (though CoQ10 isn’t in APEX, PQQ helps increase CoQ10 levels naturally) and alongside antioxidants like pine bark. Anecdotally, biohackers who use PQQ often report improved mental energy and recall over weeks of use. Mechanistically, PQQ can induce the production of nerve growth factor too, adding to the neurotrophic milieu with Lion’s Mane[30].

Stress Resilience & Dopamine Support: L-Tyrosine (1 g), Rhodiola rosea (300 mg)

This pair of ingredients bolsters resilience to stress and maintains neurotransmitter balance. Tyrosine fuels dopamine production under pressure, while Rhodiola acts as an adaptogen to normalize the body's stress response.

Cognitive demands and stimulants can put the body’s stress response into play, so APEX includes agents to bolster resilience to stress and maintain neurotransmitter balance. L-Tyrosine is a direct nutritional precursor to the catecholamine neurotransmitters: dopamine, norepinephrine, and epinephrine. During prolonged wakefulness or acute stress (cold, high workload, etc.), the brain’s dopamine/norepinephrine levels get depleted, which impairs cognitive function (working memory, cognitive flexibility). Tyrosine supplementation has been shown to improve cognitive and motor performance under stress by replenishing these neurotransmitters[31]. For instance, military research found that taking tyrosine (approx. 2 g) before periods of cold exposure or sleep deprivation preserved reaction time and reasoning abilities better than placebo. Another study in healthy adults doing a mentally demanding task in the face of distraction showed tyrosine improved performance, presumably by boosting dopamine in the prefrontal cortex. At 1,000 mg, APEX’s dose is solid (typical range 500–2,000 mg). It should elevate plasma tyrosine sufficiently to be taken up into the brain and converted to dopamine/norepi as needed. This is especially useful in a stimulant-containing stack: caffeine causes the release of catecholamines, and tyrosine can help sustain that supply so that focus remains sharp and mood doesn’t dip once initial neurotransmitters are expended. Anecdotally, users note that tyrosine gives a feeling of being “on” and motivated, particularly in stressful multi-tasking situations or when sleep-deprived – exactly the scenarios where research confirms its benefits.

Rhodiola rosea is a classic adaptogen herb with extensive use in stress and fatigue management. At 300 mg (standardized to ≥3% rosavins, ≥1% salidroside), APEX provides a clinically-studied dose. Rhodiola works primarily by modulating the HPA (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal) axis – it can normalize cortisol release and has been shown to reduce stress, mental fatigue, and improve well-being under strain[25]. A meta-analysis of Rhodiola trials in fatigue found significant improvements in fatigue, mood, and concentration compared to placebo. One notable study in physicians on night shift showed that 170 mg Rhodiola extract improved cognitive performance (specifically, associative thinking) and reduced fatigue after just two weeks. Another trial in students during an exam period found those taking Rhodiola had better concentration and lower mental fatigue than placebo. Mechanistically, Rhodiola’s rosavins may influence monoamine levels (serotonin, dopamine) by inhibiting their breakdown, and salidroside has neuroprotective effects (reducing inflammation and oxidative damage from stress). It’s also been noted to increase levels of BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) in animal models, suggesting it can support brain plasticity under chronic stress. In APEX, Rhodiola likely helps users maintain peak cognitive performance even as the day’s demands accumulate – it fights the “burnout” feeling and stabilizes mood and motivation. It may also synergize with tyrosine: Rhodiola prevents excessive cortisol (which can deplete dopamine via feedback loops), while tyrosine ensures dopamine and norepinephrine synthesis is fueled – together promoting a balanced, resilient stress response.
