What Are Single-Strain Probiotics?

Probiotics are living microorganisms that help support the body when ingested. Each probiotic is a member of a genus (e.g., family), species, and strain. While different probiotics might be classified within the same genus, or even species, different individual members, which we call strains, may behave differently and, therefore, exert different health benefits. Single-strain probiotics provide one targeted probiotic strain.

Single-Strain

One targeted probiotic strain with specific, often highly researched benefits, like Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG for digestive and immune health.

Multi-Strain

Combines multiple strains of probiotic bacteria, potentially offering broader effects or targeting multiple aspects of health.

Single-Strain vs. Multi-Strain Probiotics

Both single-strain and multi-strain probiotics have benefits. While single strains are more widely studied for their targeted health benefits and may not have to compete with other strains, multi-strain products offer the potential for a broader range of effects.

When deciding the best for you, consider the strain(s) quality and the CFUs present. The most important factor to consider is the quality of the probiotic strains. Look at the available clinical trials behind the strains to help you select the right strain (or strains).

The research today shows that multi-strain probiotics are often not more effective than single-strain probiotics.

When evaluating single- versus multi-strain probiotics, an equally important factor is the number of colony-forming units or CFUs. This refers to the number of live and active microorganisms that can be found in each serving of the probiotic you are taking. It's the way that live probiotics are measured.

A reputable brand using a well-researched strain will provide the number of CFUs throughout shelf life, not just at the time of manufacturing, to ensure maximum effectiveness.

Multi-Strain Probiotic Benefits

Some key potential benefits of multi-strain probiotics exist; however, more science is still needed to confirm these assumptions.

Potentially Broader Effects

With multiple strains of bacteria, a product can have multiple mechanisms of action that provide a broader range of beneficial effects.

It is essential to choose a transparent brand that includes all the strains used and the reason for using them. More is not necessarily better. So, rather than randomly including a bunch of different strains in a product, you want to ensure that each strain is backed by controlled, human clinical studies that tested the same amount per serving delivered by the product you are purchasing.

Potential Additive or Synergistic Effects

Because multiple probiotic strains can potentially have different mechanisms of action, some people believe they may have additive or synergistic effects.

For example, if you include two strains that function via the same mechanism of action, do you get 1+1 = 2?

Or, if you include strains with different mechanisms of action, do you get 1+1 = 3? While it's great to consider, we don't know enough to prove whether this benefit exists.

Probiotic Strains FAQ

How many strains of probiotics are there?

There are thousands of probiotic strains, many lacking sufficient scientific evidence. This is why it is so important to prioritize well-researched strains such as Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG.

Does the number of strains of probiotics matter?

We do not have sufficient scientific evidence to support that more probiotic strains are better. More research is needed to test individual strains against combinations of the same strains. For example, you need to evaluate Strain 1, Strain 2, and Strain 1 + Strain 2 against a placebo to determine whether providing multiple strains is better. This can get complicated, especially as you add more strains!

What strain of probiotics is best?

There is no single probiotic strain that is considered "best." You want to choose a strain(s) that is clinically effective for your health needs.

Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG is an excellent choice when evaluating a probiotic to support digestive and immune health*. It is an extensively studied probiotic strain, in nearly 2000 scientific studies, including over 300 human clinical trials.
